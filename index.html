Key Findings & Patterns
🚫
Zero CRM Deals Closed
Of the 22 CRM deals created in September, not a single one has closed won. All 16 resolved CRM deals ended as Closed Lost. 6 remain active in early stages.
♻️
Inherited / Legacy Deals
Multiple deals were flagged in notes as "Inherited from Ray" and noted as opportunities that "should have never been a deal." These were created in HubSpot as part of rep transitions, not as new opportunities.
📉
High-Volume Rep Loss Concentration
Larry Marino carried 8 deals — 7 of which closed lost. All 7 lost deals were Agent Only product. Janek Pfeifer had 8 deals: 7 lost, 1 won. Two reps account for 14 of 33 losses (42%).
🏫
Deal Quality at Creation
Notes reference small institution budgets, government department funding issues, prospects already using competitors ("college vine"), and deals that died after first discovery call — suggesting some deals lacked proper qualification before creation.
⚔️
Competitive Displacement Challenges
Several notes cite competitive losses: existing vendor relationships, "stiff-armed demo" situations, and RFP processes already underway (e.g., Southern Utah University, Western Michigan). September may have had a high volume of late-cycle competitive entries.
✅
Active Pipeline Still Has Upside
11 deals remain active across Stages 1–3, representing ~$1.07M in potential. Two deals are at Stage 3: Contract (Louisiana State at $150K, Tulane at $123K). Lucas Brewer's two deals are both still active.
